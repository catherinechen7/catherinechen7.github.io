History

History of the Group

Founding

Founded during winter study

Ephoria was founded during Winter Study in 1979. At this time there were only two a cappella groups on campus: the Octet (all-male) and the Ephlats (co-ed). Kristan Zimmerman ‘81 had the idea to create an all-women’s group, and with her roommate Kyle Hodgkins ‘81 held auditions for the first women’s a cappella group at Williams College. According to Kyle, “We sort of looked around and realized that the Octet was having all the fun, and there wasn't a women's version...so we decided to make one.”

Naming

Named after the Andrews Sisters

The original musical reference for the group was the Andrews Sisters, but they quickly branched out. The original repertoire included songs like “How About You?,” ”Java Jive,” “Mood Indigo,” and “Breaking Up is Hard to Do” among others. Ephoria’s first concert was on April 21st, 1979. That spring they also sang with the Octet on Chapin steps to celebrate 10 years of coeducation. From there the group gave multiple concerts a year, sang at alumni events, and performed at the Log. This first year they also took a trip to Boston where they performed at Faneuil Hall.

Concert Traditions

Skits, joke numbers, and duets

Building on the laid back, fun loving vibe of the original group, Ephoria members in the late 1980s performed skits or joke numbers in their concerts. One year they collaborated with the Octet on “Summer Loving” from Grease with the words rewritten to be about Williams (i.e. “Showed her my Mac, she adjusted my font”).

Today

No end to the ugly cry

The original members never imagined the group would outlast them, but 40 years later we’re still going strong. We’ve continued the tradition of hosting a cappella groups from other colleges, going on road trips during the spring, and most importantly we share a love of singing and for each other. Kyle sums it up well saying “My favorite memories are... about the rehearsal time, hanging out, singing and laughing till we snorted and ugly cried.”
