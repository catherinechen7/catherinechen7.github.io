FAQ

2020-2021

Q: Is Ephoria still singing together this year?

A: Definitely! Ephoria is fully committed to singing together this year under the new social distancing guidelines. Rehearsals will look different this year, and we’re still working to figure out exactly what that means, but we will work together as a group to create the best musical experience possible for our members.

Q: Will Ephoria be able to perform this year?

A: While the types of concerts we’ve previously held are not possible given social distancing constraints, we will get creative to make sure we can still perform this year. Whether that means we hold all concerts outdoors or make recordings and have a big music release night, we will find a way to share our music with our friends and families.

Q: Will Ephoria still hold social events this year?

A: 100%. Given new social distancing constraints, building and maintaining a sense of community is at the core of our mission for the year. We’ve already begun brainstorming a ton of fun ways to connect virtually throughout the semester, and we’ll plan distancing-friendly events like picnics and outdoor jam sessions for members who elected to do an on-campus semester. Weekly “Ephoria dates” have become a group tradition, and we will certainly keep these going, too.

Q: What will rehearsals look like?

A: Most music learning will happen outside of rehearsal this semester. We will be recording practice tracks to support our members who don’t read music, and will focus rehearsals on spending time together. We might attempt smaller physical meetings (such as in parts or in quartets) to practice singing together, with masks and social distancing, but will adjust to circumstances.

Q: How big of a time commitment is Ephoria?

A: In previous semesters, we’ve rehearsed 5 hrs/week. This semester, we’re going to talk as a group once we’ve welcomed new members to evaluate our situation and decide whether or not we want to change our rehearsal frequency for the semester.


Auditions

Q: What do auditions look like?

A: This year, we’ll be holding virtual auditions over zoom. We’ll ask you to sing a verse and a chorus of a song of your choice, and we’ll also lead you through a series of vocal warm ups and pitch-matching exercises. Callbacks will be in person if you are on campus and over zoom if you aren’t on campus.

Q: What should I have prepared for my audition?

A: Please prepare a chorus and a verse of any song that you think showcases your voice well. We’ll ask you to sing this acapella at your audition!
